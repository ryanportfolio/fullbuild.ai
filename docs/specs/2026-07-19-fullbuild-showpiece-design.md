# fullbuild.ai Showpiece Design Specification

**Status:** Direction authorized; conceit independently rated `PROBABLE`. Prototype and rendered QA must confirm it.

**Audience:** Recruiters and hiring teams evaluating AI-focused candidates.

**Primary outcome:** Make complete product judgment—idea, design, engineering, shipment—inspectable through real evidence.

**Official tagline:** **idea → design → engineering → shipped**

## 1. Fact boundary

Confirmed facts:

- The domain and project name are `fullbuild.ai`.
- This is a portfolio showcase.
- The fixed tagline is `idea → design → engineering → shipped`.
- The first-release audience is recruiters and hiring teams for AI-focused roles.
- The site must avoid recognizable AI-slop design and receive at least three full rendered iteration passes.

Unknown and therefore forbidden to invent:

- Owner name, biography, exact target titles, résumé, email, social/profile links, location, work authorization, client work, testimonials, external projects, usage metrics, performance results, production URL, and deployment status.

Case Study 00 may prove only the design and construction of `fullbuild.ai`. It may not imply broader product experience without supplied evidence.

## 2. Load-bearing conceit: Intent, Conserved

One product identity and one traceable proof lineage move through four lifecycle states. Requirements may pivot; commitments become costlier to reverse; material changes remain visible.

The visual anchor is one deterministic **proof object** rendered through four representations:

1. **Idea — signal:** an open boundary, sparse vertices, unresolved extent.
2. **Design — contour:** a continuous silhouette and editable control cage.
3. **Engineering — system:** indexed topology, seams, constraints, and dependency structure.
4. **Shipped — proof:** a closed surface and outbound evidence. This treatment remains locked until real deployment proof exists.

The conceit must answer layout, color, type, motion, navigation, content, and review decisions. If the prototype reads merely as “a morphing 3D mesh,” the conceit has failed.

## 3. Binding constraint contract

1. Exactly four semantic lifecycle states, always ordered and labeled with the official tagline.
2. Z-depth means lifecycle progress only. No decorative depth.
3. Signal vermilion means explicit human decision affecting intent. It is not a generic CTA, hover, or decoration color.
4. Tentative structure is dashed; committed structure is solid; unavailable proof is visibly locked rather than faked.
5. Every public claim links to a real brief, artifact, source file, test, rendered behavior, or deployed URL.
6. One product identity persists, but documented human decisions may change its topology. Never hide a mesh swap or call changed requirements “conserved.”
7. One camera axis, one easing family, one signature alignment. No ambient particles, cursor follower, perpetual orbit, blob field, scroll hijack, fake terminal, or unrelated parallax.
8. DOM contains all meaning and actions. Canvas is aria-hidden enhancement.
9. Reduced motion, forced colors, JavaScript-off, context loss, weak GPU, and narrow screens retain complete content and stage semantics.
10. Close-reading wit budget: at most one truthful marginal note per viewport.
11. No fake projects, logos, testimonials, charts, benchmarks, metrics, code, or shipped claims.
12. Three minimum critique/fix/re-render passes are required before approval.

## 4. Information architecture

### Header

- `fullbuild.ai` wordmark.
- Direct anchors: `case 00`, `method`, `verification`.
- No résumé/contact/source link until a real destination exists.

### Hero: identity and promise

- One H1 that names the end-to-end capability in plain language.
- Exact official tagline visible without JavaScript.
- Current Case 00 status shown honestly as `design` or `engineering`; never `shipped` before proof.
- Immediate route to inspect Case Study 00.
- Proof object beside or behind content without reducing contrast or interaction.

### Case Study 00: building fullbuild.ai

Show four lifecycle stages in semantic DOM. Each stage includes:

- one real source/artifact;
- one consequential decision;
- one rejected alternative or constraint;
- one verification status.

Initial honest artifacts:

- Idea: portfolio purpose, recruiter audience, official tagline, anti-slop requirement.
- Design: four concept candidates, selected conceit, constraint contract, visual system.
- Engineering: static architecture, deterministic geometry, shader/state implementation, tests, accessibility/performance instrumentation.
- Shipped: locked pending real production URL and release evidence.

### Decision ledger

Expose a small number of high-leverage decisions: why the conceit exists, why raw machinery replaces a 3D library, why no project gallery exists yet, and why shipment is gated.

### System anatomy

Explain DOM-first progressive enhancement, deterministic proof geometry, fallback behavior, reduced motion, and verification harness. Link only to paths or URLs safe and available to the public build.

### Revision and verification ledger

Display real iteration pass IDs, defect counts, fixed items, and test state only when generated from actual records. Never hard-code flattering counts.

### Footer

Show build state and honest missing-publication status. Add owner identity, résumé, contact, professional links, source, and production proof only when supplied and verified.

## 5. Visual system

### Palette

| Token | Value | Meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| Carbon | `#0B0D0C` | Primary field |
| Bone | `#F1EBDC` | Primary text/light field |
| Graphite | `#242927` | Secondary surfaces on light |
| Nickel | `#9BA89F` | Secondary text/structure on dark |
| Signal vermilion | `#FF6038` | Explicit human decision only |

No CSS background gradients. WebGL tonal variation comes from geometry, normals, depth, and one computed light.

### Typography

Preferred specimen: Recursive variable type family from the official OFL source.

- Prose: proportional, linear, moderate weight.
- Display: proportional, slightly expressive only where intent is introduced.
- Metadata: same family moved toward mono; no separate fake-terminal aesthetic.
- Large lines: 10–12 words maximum.
- Body measure: 55–68 characters.
- System fallbacks must preserve layout and meaning.

Typography remains a prototype gate until real WOFF2 size, axes, glyphs, anti-aliasing, and browser rendering are inspected.

### Composition

- Asymmetric longitudinal section, not centered template symmetry.
- Desktop: content and proof object share a split field; four-stage rail remains visible.
- Mobile: normal document flow with discrete stage stills; no pinned scene.
- Rounded containers only where interaction or clipping requires them. Default geometry is squared, cut, or rule-defined.

## 6. 3D and interaction machinery

- Generate geometry deterministically from a stable seed (`fullbuild.ai/case-00`).
- Keep one indexed base mesh and stage-specific position/normal/state data.
- Raw WebGL2 uses a vertex shader for state interpolation and a fragment shader for computed lighting/line semantics.
- Request frames only during input or transition; sleep when settled.
- Scroll progress may select stages but never hijack native scrolling.
- Stage buttons and arrow keys set the same state as scrolling.
- Pointer inspection tilt is clamped to 3° and only active on capable pointers.
- WebGL compile/link errors and context loss immediately reveal the fallback.
- The shipped visual state is code-gated by verified deployment data.

## 7. Motion contract

- One easing family: `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` and time-scaled variants.
- Motion explains commitment, alignment, and stage causality only.
- No content waits on entrance animation.
- Reduced motion uses discrete state changes with no interpolation, parallax, or camera travel.
- No idle animation after the scene settles.

## 8. Accessibility contract

- WCAG 2.2 AA target.
- Semantic landmarks, one H1, skip link, logical heading order, visible focus, and full keyboard access.
- No color-only, depth-only, or motion-only meaning.
- Core page usable without JavaScript and at 200% text/400% zoom.
- Canvas is aria-hidden; adjacent DOM labels describe current state.
- Forced colors hides nonessential canvas and preserves native controls.
- Reduced motion renders complete discrete states.
- Manual keyboard, reading-order, zoom, and reduced-motion checks required in addition to automated checks.

## 9. Performance contract

Prototype budgets:

- Critical HTML + CSS: ≤35 KB Brotli.
- Initial JavaScript: ≤90 KB Brotli.
- Font subset: ≤80 KB WOFF2.
- Zero above-fold texture downloads.
- Desktop mesh: ≤80k triangles; mobile enhancement: ≤24k triangles.
- Draw calls: ≤12; GPU memory target: ≤64 MB.
- No render-blocking third party.
- LCP ≤2.5 s, INP ≤200 ms, CLS ≤0.1 when measured under a documented environment.

Publish no performance number until the measurement command, date, environment, and result exist.

## 10. Acceptance and eval harness

Automated checks must verify:

- exact tagline and four ordered stages;
- one canonical stage data source;
- deterministic geometry for stable seed;
- stage interpolation endpoints;
- shipped state locked without deployment proof;
- zero forbidden placeholder/fake-claim patterns;
- essential content present in static HTML;
- JS/CSS/font/mesh budgets;
- no missing internal anchors or assets.

Rendered checks must cover at least:

- `1440×900`, `1024×768`, and `390×844`;
- JavaScript on/off;
- reduced motion;
- keyboard-only navigation;
- WebGL success and forced fallback;
- 200% text zoom;
- full-page and above-fold screenshots.

## 11. Required iteration passes

Each pass reviews the final rendered output, produces concrete defects plus one conceit-deepening opportunity, fixes accepted defects, re-renders, and reruns authoritative checks.

1. **Recruiter comprehension:** identity, promise, proof path, scan speed, honest evidence.
2. **Craft:** typography, spacing, composition, palette, 3D geometry, shader quality, motion timing, responsive behavior.
3. **Anti-slop and resilience:** trope detection, semantic consistency, accessibility, reduced motion, fallback, performance, factual audit.

Stop only when the latest pass reports no factual errors or contract violations. Opportunities may remain only when explicitly deferred with reason.

## 12. Release blockers

- Owner identity and recruiter pathway unclear.
- Any implied shipment without reachable deployment proof.
- Fabricated project, metric, logo, testimonial, benchmark, source, or test result.
- Core content or navigation unavailable without WebGL/JavaScript.
- Generic AI-site visual trope replaces the subject-derived contract.
- Mobile, keyboard, reduced-motion, fallback, or contrast failure.
- Performance budgets unmeasured or missed without documented exception.
- Fewer than three completed fix/re-render critique passes.
